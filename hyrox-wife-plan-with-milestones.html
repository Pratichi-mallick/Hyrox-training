Head-to-Toe Warm-Up

Growth starts with a good warm-up! Complete every exercise in order before each session. Takes 8–10 minutes. Never skip it.

Full Body
Jumping Jacks
60 reps
Feet together, arms at sides. Jump feet out wide as arms swing overhead, then return. Steady rhythm. Raises heart rate and warms the whole body before anything else.
Neck Work
Side to Side
12 reps
Sitting or standing tall. Slowly move your head from centre to the right side, return to centre, then left. Never force - maintain a level head position.
Up and Down
12 reps
Chin slowly drops to chest, then gently lifts to look up. Controlled movement — this is mobilisation, not a stretch.
Tilting Side to Side
12 reps
Keep chin level and tilt the whole head ear to shoulder, pausing briefly at each end. 12 reps total.
Neck Rotation Clockwise–Anticlockwise
6-6 reps
Slow full neck circles. 6 clockwise, 6 anticlockwise. If you feel any sharp pain, reduce the range.
Shoulder Work
Arm Swing / Rotation Clockwise–Anticlockwise
6-6 reps
Arms out to the sides. Big windmill circles forward for 6 reps, then backward for 6. Increase range gradually. Warms the rotator cuff before carries and overhead work.
Torso Work
Torso Side to Side Rotation L–R
6-6 reps
Feet shoulder-width, arms crossed or out. Rotate upper body left then right — hips stay square, only the torso turns. Mobilises the thoracic spine.
Torso Side to Side Reach L–R
6-6 reps
Standing tall, reach one arm up and over to the opposite side — a lateral side bend. 6 each side. Stretches the lats and obliques.
Torso Step and Reach L–R
6-6 reps
Step to the side with one foot as you reach the opposite arm overhead and across, creating a full lateral stretch through the body. Step back and alternate.
Hip Work
Hip Rotation Clockwise–Anticlockwise
6-6 reps
Hands on hips, feet shoulder-width. Draw big slow circles with your hips — like a hula hoop. 6 clockwise, 6 anticlockwise. Mobilises the hip joint before squats and lunges.
Glute + Leg Work
Forward Bending Hamstring Stretch — Forward-Backward
6-6 reps
Stand hip-width. Move 1 leg forward, hinge at the hips, hands reaching toward the floor, then return. 6 reps forward, 6 as you step slightly back. Keep knees soft. Dynamic hamstring and glute activation.
Bodyweight Squat (slow + supported)
12 reps
Hold a wall or stick if needed. Lower slowly (3 seconds down), pause at the bottom, rise. Focus on depth and keeping heels flat — this is mobility work, not strength work.
Bodyweight Lunge (slow + supported)
12 reps
Step forward, lower the back knee toward the floor slowly, return. Alternate legs. 12 reps total. Use a wall or stick for balance. Prepares hip flexors and quads for running and lunge stations.
World's Greatest Stretch (elbow-to-instep) L–R
6-6 reps
Step into a deep lunge. Drop your same-side elbow to the inside of your front foot. Hold 2s. Then rotate that arm up to the ceiling, following with your eyes. Return and switch. 6 each side. One of the best full-body mobility moves you can do.
Ankle Work
Ankle Circles Clockwise–Anticlockwise
6-6 reps
Seated or standing on one foot. Draw slow full circles with the ankle. 6 clockwise, 6 anticlockwise per foot. Critical for running mechanics and calf raise form.
Tip Toe Walks (slow) Forward–Backward
6-6 reps × 2
Rise onto your toes and walk forward slowly for 6 steps, then backward for 6. 2 lengths. Activates the calves and trains ankle stability.
Heel Walks (slow) Forward–Backward
6-6 reps × 2
Lift your toes off the floor and walk forward on your heels for 6 steps, then backward for 6. 2 lengths. Strengthens the tibialis anterior — important for shin splint prevention when running.
Side Ankle Walks (slow) Forward–Backward
6-6 reps × 2
Walk sideways on the outer edges of your feet for 6 steps, then inner edges for 6. 2 lengths. Builds lateral ankle stability.
Heel to Toe Rocks
12 reps
Standing, rock your weight slowly forward onto your toes, then back onto your heels. Warms the full ankle range of motion before running or plyometrics.
Core + Back Work
Core Engagement Practice
12 reps × 2
Lie on your back, knees bent. Breathe in to expand your ribcage 360°. As you exhale, brace your core as if bracing for a punch — ribcage down, lower back gently pressing toward the floor. Hold 3–5 seconds. This teaches you how to maintain a stable spine under load — the foundation for every exercise in this programme.
Dead Bug
12 reps × 2
On your back, arms pointing to ceiling, knees at 90°. Maintaining your core brace, slowly lower your right arm and left leg toward the floor — pause just above — return. Alternate sides. Non-negotiable before every session.
Glute Bridges
12 reps × 2
On your back, feet flat, hip-width. Drive through your heels to lift your hips until your body forms a straight line from shoulders to knees. Squeeze hard at the top for 1 second, lower slowly. Activates glutes before squats, lunges and runs.
Mountain Climbers (slow)
12 reps × 2
In a plank position, hands under shoulders. Slowly drive one knee toward your chest, return, then the other. Keep hips level — do not let them rise. This is slow and controlled — not the cardio version.
💡 This warm-up runs head to toe on purpose — ankles and feet last, core last of all, so you are fully warm before the most demanding activation work. Add any other exercise depending on stiffness in different part of the body
// after warm-up only

Beginner Plyometrics

Complete all exercises each session. Optional ones at the bottom if time permits. Rest 60s between sets. Always finish the full warm-up first.

⚡ Never skip the warm-up and jump straight to these. Cold muscles and explosive movement do not mix.
Pogo Jumps
How to
2 × 24 reps
Stand feet hip-width, arms slightly out for balance. Bounce on the balls of your feet in a rapid, small, rhythmic jump — like a pogo stick. Keep your ankles stiff and ground contact time as short as possible. Land softly through the ball of the foot. Builds ankle stiffness and calf power for running.
Pogo Side to Side Jumps
How to
2 × 24 reps
Same pogo bouncing action but lateral — hop side to side over an imaginary line. Both feet land and leave together. Keep hops small and quick. Land with a soft knee and stable ankle. Builds lateral ankle stability and reactive power.
Pogo Front and Back Jumps
How to
2 × 24 reps
Pogo bouncing action forward and backward over an imaginary line. Both feet together, quick small hops. Stay on the balls of your feet throughout. Directly relevant to the burpee broad jump push-off and landing.
Single Leg Taps with Weight Plate
How to
2 × 24 reps
Place a small weight plate (2.5–5 kg) flat on the floor. Balance on one leg. Tap the top of the plate with the ball of your free foot — quick light taps. Keep a soft bend in the standing knee. Hold something for balance at first. Trains single-leg balance and proprioception under load.
Single Leg Step-Up with Weight Plate
How to
2 × 24 reps
Hold a weight plate (5 kg) at chest or sides. Step onto a low box with one foot, drive through that heel to stand tall at the top, step back down. 24 reps per leg or alternating. Builds single-leg strength for running and the sled drive.
Broad Jump
How to
2 × 12 reps
Feet hip-width. Bend knees, swing arms back, then explode forward — jump as far as you can. Land with soft knees and hips, absorbing through both legs. Walk back and reset. Direct prep for the Hyrox burpee broad jump station. Focus on the landing — absorb first, distance second.
Calf Raise with Yoga Block
How to
2 × 12 reps
Stand with the balls of your feet on the edge of a yoga block (or step), heels hanging off. Lower your heels below the block for a full stretch, then rise onto your toes as high as possible. Hold 1s at the top. Lower slowly (3 seconds). Builds calf strength and Achilles resilience for running.
Box Step-Up — optional if time permits
How to
2 × 12 reps
Step onto a low box with one foot, drive through that heel to bring your full body up, step back down. Alternate legs. Keep torso upright. Builds single-leg power and stability — relevant to running stride and sled push drive.
Lateral Bound — optional if time permits
How to
2 × 12 reps
Stand on one foot. Push sideways off that foot and land on the opposite foot — like a skater. Pause and balance before the next rep. Focus on a stable landing before increasing width. Builds lateral power and ankle stability.
💡 The pogo jumps train ankle stiffness and reactive power — qualities that directly reduce fatigue when running between Hyrox stations. Do these consistently and you will feel the difference on race day.
